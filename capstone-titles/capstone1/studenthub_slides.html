Slide 2 · The Problem
What actually breaks — and for whom

Five Real Failures on Our Campus

Costly mobile load for study/research
Students spend ₱50–100/week on mobile data just to research and access online academic resources on campus.
No reliable, affordable campus WiFi
The existing campus ISP lines are restricted and not open to the general student population, forcing reliance on telco SIMs.
IT projects and solutions get shelved on localhost
Excellent student-built applications and systems die immediately after defense due to lack of hosting servers, domains, or budgets.
Auditable trail gap in org collections
Student organization dues, fines, and collections are strictly cash-only, leading to lost receipts and disputes.
No host infrastructure for campus apps
Local innovations like event voting, cashless canteen, and queue systems have no server infrastructure to run on.

Slide 3 · Proposed Solution
Self-sustaining edge infrastructure

Two Services, One Local Server

StudentHub is a self-sustaining campus edge infrastructure. It pairs a PisoWiFi gateway (IGP revenue to fund the ISP line) with a Dokploy app server (local cloud hosting for students) on a single physical machine.
Side A · IGP Revenue
PisoWiFi Gateway
Provides tiered internet access via physical coins/bills or GCash. Generates revenue to self-sustain ISP bills and hardware costs.
Side B · Local Cloud
Dokploy App Server
Local PaaS ("Vercel alternative") running on Proxmox. IT students deploy web apps and databases locally with zero hosting cost or internet dependency.
Unified Payment API & Closed-Loop Wallet
Integrates physical hardware (coin/bill via ESP32) & digital channels (GCash/Maya via Xendit API). Operates as a Closed-Loop Utility Wallet (exemp. from BSP licensing): auto-cash-ins, but manual, documented vouchers for COA audit compliance.
Compliance & Risk: BSP Circular 1166 exempt. COA audit compliance via manual disbursements. Tech risk mitigated by utilizing Proxmox, Dokploy, and JuanFi.

Slide 5 · Research Novelty & Gaps
What this fills — in literature and practice

Why This Is a Research Contribution, Not Just a Project

SUC Server Infrastructure Gap
Most CHED institutions have procured servers through government grants — but they sit underutilized due to PAR liability and lack of sysadmin staff. No published model exists for how a student-led body can legitimately operate edge infrastructure within a SUC.
No Zero-Cost Hosting for IT Students
Philippine SUC students lack access to deployment infrastructure. No study has proposed a campus-local PaaS model (via Dokploy on Proxmox) as an alternative to commercial cloud for BSIT capstone projects.
No Campus-Embedded Micro-Payment API
Org fund collection still relies on physical cash. No campus-embedded micro-payment API has been documented in Philippine academic literature as a transparent, auditable alternative.
No Self-Sustaining Funding Model for Student Org Infrastructure
Student orgs that attempt tech projects face a recurring funding wall — hardware depreciates, ISP bills recur, and org budgets reset yearly. No documented model exists where a student org IGP directly funds and sustains its own campus IT infrastructure through built-in revenue (PisoWiFi → server costs), eliminating dependency on institutional budget allocation.
Slide 6 · Scope & Target Clients
Project Boundary & Adoption Strategy

Scope, Limitations & Target Clients

In Scope
  • Physical boundaries of target prospect campus (e.g., ISPSC Tagudin)
  • PisoWiFi revenue loop funding the ISP line
  • Dokploy local PaaS app deployment
  • Closed-Loop Wallet: Xendit API digital cash-in, manual COA-compliant voucher cash-out
  • ML bandwidth usage prediction
  • IoT node health mapping
Out of Scope
  • Integration with target institution's main network / MIS
  • Multi-campus rollout (future recommendations)
  • External internet access for hosted apps
  • Open-Loop Wallet or BSP EMI licensing (exempt under BSP Circular 1166)
  • Automated digital payouts/disbursements (COA regulatory compliance restriction)
Adoption Clients
Primary
CAS Provincial Org (CASPO)
Oversees CAS organizations. Mandates the project as an IGP-funded pilot, creating a province-wide template.
Prospect Pilot
ISPSC Tagudin Campus
Proposed pilot campus. Target for local app hosting and student WiFi without institutional budget allocation.
Private Schools & Colleges
External replication sites facing WiFi sustainability and local project hosting issues.
