Section 2.0 · Challenges
§ 03 / 11

The Three Administrative Bottlenecks

01
Disjointed Journeys
No dynamic cross-department handoff. A student waits for an hour at Registrar only to discover they must pay a deficiency at the Cashier first, forcing them to exit, line up at the Cashier, and restart the Registrar line.
Impact: Compounded waiting times
02
Vocal announcement Fatigue
Clerks must shout names or manually click basic standalone numeric counters. In noisy corridors, students miss their calls, leading to skipped tokens, backlogs, and chaotic crowd clusters around service windows.
Impact: Administrative friction & confusion
03
High Infrastructure Costs
Commercial queue systems cost thousands of dollars and depend on proprietary hardware. Existing cloud-only solutions fail completely during campus internet outages, paralyzing operations during critical enrollment windows.
Impact: Budget drain & zero local failover

Research Objectives

01
Build Offline-First LAN Orchestrator
Design a local-first queue system running on cheap SBCs using Laravel Reverb and SQLite to ensure operations continue during WAN outages.

Implement FlowEngine Routing
Create a state-machine based routing engine supporting priority ratio lanes, multi-service workflows, and clerk desk transfers.

Section 6.0 · Limits
§ 08 / 11

System Scope & Boundaries

Technical In-Scope

Multi-Surface Views: Dedicated Kiosk, Display, Staff Dashboard, and Admin interfaces with responsive styles.
Flow Engine & Dynamic Routing: Ordered steps, station transfer transitions, and ratio priority lanes.
ML-driven Wait Prediction: Predict service times dynamically using historical transaction logs.
Indoor Locator Mapping: Leaflet-based campus visual routing paths for desk transfers on mobile.
Audit Compliance: Immutable append-only TransactionLog tracking all token transitions.

Out-of-Scope Limitations

Native Mobile Applications: No App Store downloads. Handled exclusively via responsive URLs locked to client browsers.
WAN queueing when offline: Queueing when isolated is strictly local to the campus LAN; global cloud updates pause until link restoration.
Hardware manufacturing: The proposal is for the software orchestrator; Raspberry Pi/Orange Pi hardware procurement is a dependency.
