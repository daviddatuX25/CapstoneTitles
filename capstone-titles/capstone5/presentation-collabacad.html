CollabAcad · Context & Hook
Section 2 — Problem Space
Structural Failures in Campus Communication

The Mess vs. The CollabAcad Resolution

Boundary Collapse

Official announcements, files, and queries are scattered across personal social media accounts (Messenger GCs). Teachers and student leaders face continuous notifications and role-identity distress outside work hours.

Representative Inequity

Student voting on fees, policies, and elections uses raw headcounts. Large colleges (like CAS with 1,200 students) arithmetically swamp small colleges (like CTE with 300 students), silencing minority voices.

Discourse Suppression

Students stay silent about critical issues (e.g. facility problems, administrative complaints) due to fear of peer judgment, academic retaliation, or public identification.

Role-Segmented Workspace

A structured institutional portal separating personal profiles from campus functions. Segmented folders for faculty instructions, student government announcements, and course discussion forums.

Segmented Weighted Voting

An algorithm that normalizes votes by department population. Turnout weight is aggregated equally across colleges, ensuring CTE carries proportional voice against CAS.

Managed Anonymity Channels

Controlled anonymous threads protected by pre-publication keyword screens. True identities are encrypted and sealed in a separate superadmin schema, maintaining accountability.

Section 3 — Platform Architecture
Three-Tier Memory & Real-Time Data Pipeline

WebSocket Event Stream

Clients establish namespace-specific WebSocket connections (/events, /voting) via Socket.IO, achieving event broadcasts under 100ms.

L1/L2 Redis Caching

L1 holds active vote tallies in-memory; L2 caches session metadata. Cache coherence is governed by atomic Redis write-locks (MESI protocol).

L3 Durable Postgres Store

Durable data goes to PostgreSQL. Real-time voting tallies use a write-through strategy to guarantee both responsiveness and transaction durability.

Section 6 — Execution Parameters
Scope Boundaries, 12-Week Roadmap & Risks

Project Scope, Roadmap, and Risks

Scope Boundaries

  • In-Scope Auth: Role-based access for Admin, Faculty, Officers, and Students.
  • In-Scope Core: Socket.IO live ticker stream, department voting weighting, and moderated anonymous channels.
  • In-Scope Admin: Moderation review queues, Postgres RLS tables, and voter registry controls.
  • Out-of-Scope GCs: Direct integration into Facebook Messenger or third-party consumer systems (retains on-prem control).
  • Out-of-Scope Apps: Native iOS/Android apps; restricted to fully-responsive Web SPA layout to limit threat surface.

Development Phases

Phase 1: Foundation & WebSockets W1 - W4

Establish Next.js interface, Node Express backend, Socket.IO namespaces, and RBAC authentication guards.

Phase 2: App & Normalized Voting W5 - W8

Implement voting modules, department weight normalization, Redis cache-locks, and live announcement ticker feeds.

Phase 3: Moderation, Audit & k6 Load Tests W9 - W12

Integrate content pre-screening pipeline, de-anonymization RLS schemas, k6 load testing, and SUS evaluations.

System Complexity Moderate-High
